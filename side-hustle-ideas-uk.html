12 Side Hustle Ideas in the UK You Can Start This Month (2026)

The Digital Park · Updated June 2026 · Reading time: 8 minutes

Looking for a side hustle in the UK that fits around a full-time job? You are not alone. With the cost of living where it is, more people than ever want a realistic second income that does not need a big investment or a career change. This list covers 12 proven side hustle ideas UK earners are actually using in 2026, with the honest startup steps for each one so you can pick the right fit and start this month.

We have left out the get-rich-quick nonsense. Every idea here is a genuine way to make money online in the UK or locally, around the hours you already have.

The 12 side hustle ideas

1. Freelance your existing skill

If you can write, design, edit video, build spreadsheets, or manage social media, someone will pay you to do it. Freelancing is the fastest side hustle to start in the UK because you already have the skill. Set up a simple profile, send a few well-written pitches, and you can land your first paid client within weeks.

2. Sell digital templates and planners

Build a template once, sell it many times. Spreadsheets, planners, content calendars, and toolkits sell well because buyers want a finished file they can use today. This is one of the most scalable ways to make money online in the UK because there is no stock and no shipping.

3. Open an Etsy shop

Etsy is ideal for makers, designers, and anyone selling printables or handmade goods. The key is good listings and search optimisation. A clear setup plan saves you weeks of trial and error.

4. Reselling and flipping

Buy underpriced items at car boot sales, charity shops, or clearance, then resell online. Low startup cost and you learn quickly what sells. Great weekend side hustle.

5. Online tutoring

If you know a subject, a language, or a skill, you can tutor online. UK demand for tutoring stays high year round, and you set your own hours.

6. Virtual assistant work

Small businesses need help with inboxes, scheduling, and admin. If you are organised, virtual assistant work is a steady side income you can build from home.

7. Content creation and a niche channel

Build an audience around a topic you know well, then earn through sponsorships, affiliate links, or your own products. Slower to pay off, but the most scalable on this list.

8. Pet sitting and dog walking

If you are reliable and like animals, local pet care is steady, repeat work that needs almost no startup money.

9. Photography and stock images

Sell prints locally or licence stock photography online. Good if you already own a decent camera and enjoy shooting.

10. Bookkeeping for small businesses

Plenty of UK sole traders need help keeping their numbers straight. With a tidy system, this is a high-value, repeatable side hustle.

11. Print on demand

Design once, and a print partner handles printing and shipping. Good for designers who want products without holding stock.

12. Local services (gardening, cleaning, handyman)

Hands-on local services book up fast through word of mouth. Low startup cost and you can scale to a small team later.

How to choose your side hustle

Pick the one that matches three things: a skill you already have, the time you can realistically give each week, and how fast you need the money. The freelance and digital template routes pay quickest because they need the least setup. The content and channel routes pay biggest over time but take patience.

Frequently asked questions

What is the easiest side hustle to start in the UK?

Freelancing an existing skill or selling digital templates, because both need almost no upfront money and can be running within a week.

Do I need to tell HMRC about side hustle income?

Once your side income passes the trading allowance, yes, you need to declare it through a self assessment tax return. Keep records from day one so it is painless.

How much can I realistically make?

It depends on the hustle and the hours, but a consistent few hours a week on the right idea can build into a meaningful second income over a few months.
